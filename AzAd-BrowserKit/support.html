Get Help & Learn Features

Find answers to common questions and learn how to get the most out of each tool.

üé® Color Picker

How do I pick a color from a website?

‚åÑ

Click the Color Picker button in the extension popup, then click on any color on the webpage. The color will be extracted and copied to your clipboard automatically. You can change the color format (HEX, RGB, HSL) in the popup before clicking.

What color formats are supported?

‚åÑ

Color Picker supports HEX (e.g., #FF5733), RGB (e.g., rgb(255, 87, 51)), and HSL (e.g., hsl(9, 100%, 60%)) formats. You can select your preferred format from the Color Picker settings.

Can I extract colors with transparency?

‚åÑ

Yes! Color Picker supports alpha channel (transparency). When you pick a semi-transparent color, it will be saved with alpha values (e.g., rgba(255, 87, 51, 0.8) or #FF573399).

üî§ Font Inspector

How do I inspect fonts on a webpage?

‚åÑ

Open the Font Inspector from the popup and hover over any text on the page. The font details will appear showing family, size, weight, line-height, and more. Click to expand and copy the information.

Can I identify Google Fonts or custom fonts?

‚åÑ

Font Inspector will show the actual font family name as declared in the CSS. For Google Fonts, web fonts, or custom fonts, it displays the primary font name and the fallback fonts in the font stack.

What information does Font Inspector show?

‚åÑ

Font Inspector provides: Font Family (name and fallbacks), Size (in px), Weight (100-900), Line Height, Letter Spacing, Text Transform, and Font Style (italic, normal).

üîó Link Collector

How does Link Collector work?

‚åÑ

Click the Link Collector button to scan the current page and extract all links. You'll see a list of all links found. You can filter by type, remove tracking parameters, and export as CSV or copy to clipboard.

What are tracking parameters?

‚åÑ

Tracking parameters are URL parameters used to track user clicks (e.g., utm_source, utm_medium, fbclid). When you enable Remove Tracking, Link Collector strips these parameters, giving you clean URLs.

How do I export links as CSV?

‚åÑ

After collecting links, click the Export CSV button in the Link Collector popup. A CSV file with all links will be downloaded to your computer, ready to open in Excel or Google Sheets.

üìß Email Extractor

How do I extract emails from a website?

‚åÑ

Click Email Extractor in the popup. It will automatically scan the webpage and find all email addresses. The extracted emails are displayed in a list where you can copy or manage them.

Can I filter emails by domain?

‚åÑ

Yes! In Email Extractor settings, you can add domains to filter for only specific email addresses. For example, filter for only @company.com emails.

Are duplicate emails removed?

‚åÑ

Absolutely! Email Extractor automatically deduplicates the email list, so you won't have multiple copies of the same email address.

More Tools

How does Table Extractor work?

‚åÑ

Open Table Extractor and select any HTML table on the page. Click Convert to CSV and the table data will be formatted as CSV. You can then copy to clipboard or download as a file.

What is Smart Links?

‚åÑ

Smart Links automatically converts plain text URLs (like www.example.com) into clickable links. You can enable/disable it per website in the settings, or turn it off globally.

How do I use Hover Zoom?

‚åÑ

Enable Hover Zoom in settings, then hover over any image or video on a webpage. It will zoom in based on your configured scale factor. Customize the zoom level and delay in settings.

Is Password Reveal enabled by default?

‚åÑ

No, Password Reveal is disabled by default for security. To enable it, go to settings and toggle it on. When enabled, press the configured modifier key (Alt, Ctrl, Shift) over a password field to reveal it. Banking sites remain protected.

What does Text Case Tools do?

‚åÑ

Select any text on a webpage and right-click. Choose Text Case Tools to convert it to UPPERCASE, lowercase, Title Case, camelCase, snake_case, or kebab-case.

General Questions

Is AzAd BrowserKit free?

‚åÑ

Yes! AzAd BrowserKit is completely free and open-source. There are no ads, no premium features, and no hidden costs. It's available under the MIT License.

Does it work offline?

‚åÑ

Yes! Most features work completely offline. Your settings are stored locally using Chrome's storage API and synced across devices if you're signed into Chrome.

Is my data safe?

‚åÑ

Your data is completely safe. AzAd BrowserKit processes everything locally on your machine. We don't collect, store, or transmit any of your data to external servers. All processing happens in your browser.

Can I use it on other Chromium browsers?

‚åÑ

Yes! AzAd BrowserKit works on any Chromium-based browser including Chrome, Edge, Brave, Vivaldi, and Chromium. Search for "AzAd BrowserKit" in your browser's extension store.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

‚åÑ

Please visit our GitHub Issues page to report bugs or request features. Provide details about what happened and steps to reproduce the issue.

Can I contribute to the project?

‚åÑ

Absolutely! We'd love your contributions. Check our Contributing Guidelines to get started.
